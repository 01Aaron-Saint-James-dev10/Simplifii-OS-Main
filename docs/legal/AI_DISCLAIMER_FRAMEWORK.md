## LAYER 1 — First-Run Acknowledgment Modal

Shown once, immediately after signup. User must click "I understand" before proceeding.

### Modal copy

**Heading:** Before you start

**Body:**

Simplifii-OS is a thinking tool. It supports your work. It does not do it for you.

Before you continue, please understand five things:

**1. You are responsible for your own work.**  
Simplifii-OS helps you prepare, organise, draft, verify, and reflect. Every word you submit, every claim you make, every citation you use, is your responsibility. Read what you write. Check what you cite. Submit only what you genuinely understand and can defend.

**2. AI can be wrong.**  
Simplifii-OS uses AI to suggest ideas, flag missing citations, surface connections in your sources, and translate rubric language. AI makes mistakes. AI sometimes invents things that look right but are not. Every suggestion is a starting point, not a fact. Verify everything important.

**3. AI does not understand your assignment the way your teacher does.**  
Our tools translate rubrics, summarise briefs, and suggest interpretations. These are interpretations, not instructions. Your teacher, lecturer, supervisor, or marker is the only authority on what your assignment actually requires. When in doubt, ask them, not us.

**4. Your institution's academic integrity rules apply.**  
Most schools and universities require you to disclose AI assistance. Some forbid certain uses entirely. You are responsible for knowing your institution's policy and following it. Using Simplifii-OS does not change those rules.

**5. Your grades are not guaranteed.**  
Simplifii-OS cannot promise you a higher mark, a pass, or any specific outcome. Grades depend on your thinking, your effort, your teacher's judgment, and many factors outside our control. We aim to help you do your best work. We do not aim to predict, influence, or guarantee your results.

[ ] I have read the [Terms of Service](/terms) and [Privacy Policy](/privacy).

## LAYER 5 — Dedicated /ai-use Policy Page

Top edtech companies (Khan Academy with Khanmigo, Duolingo, Quillbot) now publish a standalone, plain-English page explaining how AI is used in their products. Students and parents look for this. Simplifii-OS should have one too.

### Recommended URL: `/ai-use` (link from footer + first-run modal + every contextual warning)

### Page outline

**Heading:** How Simplifii-OS uses AI

**Section 1: What we use AI for**

- Translating assignment briefs into plainer language.
- Suggesting structure, headings, or outlines.
- Drafting starting points you then edit in your own voice.
- Identifying citations in your text and matching them against your uploaded sources.
- Flagging claims that may not be supported by what you've uploaded.
- Summarising long documents you've uploaded.
- Suggesting which task to focus on next.
- Offering stretches, breaks, and focus prompts.

**Section 2: What we do not use AI for**

- We do not use AI to grade your work.
- We do not use AI to predict your grade or future performance.
- We do not use AI to make decisions about your academic standing.
- We do not use AI to surveil, track, or profile you for any third party.
- We do not use AI to write your final submission for you.

**Section 3: Which AI models we use**

Currently, Simplifii-OS uses:

- Anthropic's Claude family of models for most text reasoning tasks (briefs, suggestions, drafts).
- Local browser-based detection (no AI model required) for citation pattern matching.
- Future local-first models for offline use (planned, not yet active).

We will update this section as our model use changes.

**Section 4: Your data and AI training**

We do not use your private content (documents, drafts, notes, citations, ingested files) to train any AI model, ours or anyone else's.

When we send text to an AI provider (such as Anthropic) for processing, we send only what is needed to perform the task. We do not retain this for training. We have contracts in place with our AI providers that prohibit training on user content. Where possible, we use providers' zero-data-retention APIs.

**Section 5: What you should always verify**

- Every citation. AI can hallucinate sources that look real but do not exist.
- Every claim of fact. AI can confidently state things that are wrong.
- Every interpretation of a rubric. AI can misread tone, context, or implicit expectations.
- Every suggested rewrite. AI can flatten your voice, miss your meaning, or change your argument.

**Section 6: How to disclose AI use to your institution**

Many institutions now require disclosure of AI-assisted work. We make this easy:

- Simplifii-OS keeps a log of AI suggestions, edits, and citations within your project (the "History of Thought" feature).
- You can export an "AI Use Receipt" that lists where AI was used in your work.
- This receipt is for your records or to share with your teacher if requested.
- Submitting it to your institution is your responsibility.

**Section 7: How to report a problem**

If Simplifii-OS gives you misleading, incorrect, or harmful AI output, please tell us. Email aaron@simplifii.com.au with the subject line "AI output report" and we will investigate.

We learn from these reports. Your feedback makes the tool safer for everyone.

## What top edtech actually does (benchmark notes)

**Khan Academy / Khanmigo:** First-run modal, contextual disclaimers on every AI tutor response, separate parent dashboard with full transparency, AI made by Anthropic prominently disclosed.

**Grammarly:** AI suggestions clearly labelled, "Edit in your own voice" prompts, generative features gated behind plan upgrades, separate transparency page on data use.

**Quillbot:** AI-paraphrase results always marked, copyright and originality warnings before every export, opt-in for AI training.

**Notion AI:** Per-block AI labels, model disclosure, enterprise data-retention controls, no training on customer data (contractual guarantee).

**Microsoft Copilot for Education:** Educator dashboard, age-appropriate filters, FERPA compliance, AI prompts disclosed inline.

**ChatGPT Edu:** Admin controls, training opt-out, audit logs, integration with institutional SSO.

**What's becoming standard in 2026:**

- Per-output AI labelling (not just per-app)
- Exportable AI use logs for academic disclosure
- Clear data-training opt-out (default off)
- Plain-English /ai-use policy page
- Separate /educators or /institutions page
- Crisis resource references on any wellness feature

Simplifii-OS implementing Layers 1-7 puts you ahead of most current edtech, level with Khan Academy and ChatGPT Edu, and significantly ahead of most AI-writing tools (Quillbot, Grammarly).
