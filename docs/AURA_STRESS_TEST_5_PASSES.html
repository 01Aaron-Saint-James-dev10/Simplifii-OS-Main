Pass 1 Neurotype stress test: every diagnosis, every edge case 18 gaps found
MISSINGAutism: AURA has no spec for literal interpretation mismatches. A Tier 2 question like "what does the author mean?" will be answered literally. No handler for when a learner answers the literal question not the intended one. Fix: literal-first interpretation mode for ASD profile. AURA never assumes implied meaning is understood.
MISSINGNon-speaking and AAC users: zero spec. Learners who use Augmentative and Alternative Communication devices type slowly and non-linearly. AURA's 90-second idle trigger fires constantly. No AAC patience mode. Fix: AAC profile flag disables all idle nudges. AURA waits indefinitely.
MISSINGDyscalculia: AURA has no handler for tasks that require numerical reasoning (science, economics, data analysis). A student with dyscalculia hitting a statistics assignment needs specific scaffolding, not generic Socratic questions. Fix: quantitative-task mode with step-decomposition protocol.
MISSINGDyspraxia / DCD: affects handwriting AND typing speed AND spatial reasoning. Voice is preferred but voice is currently a toggle, not a primary default. AURA still times responses as if typing speed is normal. Fix: motor profile field. Adjusts all timing thresholds and defaults voice as primary.
MISSINGTourette syndrome: involuntary keystrokes and voice tics corrupt input. AURA treats corrupted input as the actual message. No debounce or confirmation protocol for motor tic interference. Fix: motor tic flag enables confirmation-before-action on every input.
MISSINGSelective mutism: learner cannot speak in anxiety-triggering contexts. Voice mode cannot be the only alternative to typing. Text must always remain equal. But also: AURA must never ask "why won't you use voice?" Fix: input method is always learner choice. AURA never comments on which method is used.
MISSINGHyperlexia: can decode text at high level but comprehension lags. Will read AURA's academic register responses fluently but not understand them. Persona = Literal is not enough here. Fix: comprehension-check protocol. After complex output, AURA asks "does that make sense?" and simplifies on request without judgment.
MISSINGSensory processing disorder: the UI itself may be overwhelming before AURA says a word. Sounds, motion, colour intensity. No pre-session sensory load check. Fix: sensory profile in accessibility_prefs. Motion, sound, animation all suppressible. AURA's tone becomes more minimal when sensory load is high.
WEAKADHD hyperfocus: v2 handles low-energy/idle states. It does NOT handle hyperfocus where the learner works 4 hours straight on the wrong task. AURA should notice when a learner is deep in a low-priority block while a deadline-critical block has not been started. Fix: hyperfocus redirect protocol. Gentle, non-interrupting check-in after 45 minutes on single block.
WEAKProcessing speed differences: AURA times Socratic responses. A slow processor gives the same answer a fast one does, just later. The 3-minute idle threshold treats slow processing as disengagement. Fix: processing speed profile field. Multiplies all idle thresholds by a factor (1x to 4x) based on declared processing speed.
MISSINGAcquired brain injury (ABI): fatigue, word-finding difficulty, memory fragmentation mid-session. AURA has no handler for a learner who suddenly cannot recall what they were doing 10 minutes ago. Fix: ABI mode enables session state summaries on demand. "Here is what you were working on and where you left off."
MISSINGIntellectual disability: AURA assumes a reading level above Grade 3. Primary tier goes to age 12 but some learners at any age read at Grade 1-2 level. No reading level calibration below primary default. Fix: reading level field (Grade 1-12+). All AURA output passes through a reading-level filter when set below Grade 4.
MISSINGGiftedness with twice-exceptionality (2e): a gifted-ADHD or gifted-ASD learner produces wildly uneven output. Top-percentile analysis but cannot format a reference list. AURA's scaffolding treats them as one or the other, never both. Fix: 2e profile flag. AURA scaffolds mechanics heavily, never scaffolds conceptual thinking unless requested.
MISSINGPTSD and complex trauma: flashback risk triggered by specific content in assessments (history, psychology, social work). AURA has no content-warning protocol before surfacing potentially triggering rubric content. Fix: CPTSD flag enables content advisory before AURA surfaces assessments with potentially distressing topics.
MISSINGEating disorder recovery: timed tasks, word counts, and performance metrics can be triggers. AURA's progress tracking (X% complete, X words written) may activate perfectionism spirals. Fix: metric suppression mode. On request, hides word counts, percentages, and time remaining from the UI surface.
MISSINGOCD with intrusive thoughts: doubt cycling. A learner with OCD may rewrite the same sentence 40 times because it is "not right yet." AURA has no handler for compulsive revision loops. Fix: revision loop detection. After 5 edits to the same block in under 10 minutes, AURA offers: "You have revised this section several times. It may be good enough. Want to move on?"
WEAKAnxiety disorders: AURA addresses distress reactively. It does not support a learner who enters every session already anxious. No proactive calibration for baseline anxiety state at session start. Fix: pre-session check-in option (not mandatory). "How are you feeling before we start? Scale of 1-3." Adjusts tone and scaffolding density for the session based on response.
MISSINGBorderline personality disorder (BPD): extreme reactions to perceived criticism. The AURA feedback model (lead with strength, then one improvement) may still trigger a shame spiral if the improvement feels like rejection. Fix: BPD-aware feedback mode. Improvement suggestions are framed as additions, not corrections. "One thing you could add is..." never "one thing that needs work is..."
Pass 2 Age and tier stress test: 5-year-olds to adult learners in crisis 14 gaps found
MISSINGAge 5-7 (early primary): AURA's minimum reading level assumes Grade 3+. A 5-year-old using Simplifii with a homeschool parent needs picture-supported prompts, single-word responses accepted, parent co-pilot mode. AURA cannot talk to a 5-year-old the way it talks to a Year 4 student. Fix: Early Learner mode (ages 5-7). All AURA output max 8 words per sentence. Parent co-pilot can see AURA's suggestions before they reach the child.
MISSINGLearners in custody or correctional education: no internet access, limited device time, sessions cut off mid-work. AURA assumes continuous sessions. No protocol for forced session termination. Fix: graceful exit at any point saves full state. On return, AURA summarises exactly where they stopped in one sentence.
MISSINGEnglish as additional language or dialect (EAL/D) learners: AURA writes in Australian English. An EAL/D student may need academic language modelling but in simpler sentence structures. The current Persona dial does not address L2 academic writing support. Fix: EAL/D mode. AURA models correct academic phrasing but also explains why each phrase is appropriate for the register.
MISSINGRefugee and asylum-seeker learners: may have significant trauma, interrupted education, no prior experience with digital learning tools, and English as L2 or L3. This intersects neurotype + EAL/D + trauma + digital literacy gap simultaneously. No intersectional profile path. Fix: intersectional profile builder. Multiple flags combine. AURA response rules stack, the most protective one always wins.
MISSINGAdult learners returning after decades away from education: 40+ year old returning to university. Imposter syndrome is extreme. Digital literacy may be low. AURA assumes digital native behaviour (drag and drop, keyboard shortcuts). Fix: digital literacy flag. When low, AURA explains every UI action in plain language before asking the learner to take it.
MISSINGLearners with dementia or early cognitive decline (some postgrad/mature age): memory and orientation issues within a single session. Stronger version of ABI. May ask the same question repeatedly in one session. AURA has no graceful repeat-handling that does not make the learner feel bad. Fix: repeat-question protocol. If the same question is asked twice in a session, AURA answers it the same way without referencing that it was already asked.
WEAKHomeschool parent as co-learner: v2 treats parents as account holders, not learners themselves. But some homeschool parents are learning the curriculum alongside their child (especially at secondary and postgrad level). No parent-as-learner mode. Fix: parent-learner toggle. Parent can switch from oversight mode to co-learner mode where they get the same guidance the child gets.
MISSINGLearners doing vocational education (VET/TAFE): not academic essays. Assessment formats include competency checklists, workplace portfolios, practical demonstrations, logbooks. AURA's rubric model assumes academic essay format. Fix: VET/TAFE assessment type detection. Pareto Steps and Socratic questions adapt to competency-based evidence requirements, not essay structure.
MISSINGPhD and research candidates: AURA's postgrad guidance is generic. A PhD student writing a literature review needs fundamentally different scaffolding than an undergraduate. Research methodology, theoretical framing, gap identification. No research-specific Socratic protocol. Fix: research mode for postgrad. Tier 2 Socratic questions shift to: "What gap in the literature does this address?" and "How does this position your theoretical framework?"
MISSINGLearners completing assessments in a language that is not AURA's output language: a French student in an Australian university writing in English. AURA cannot assume the learner's thinking language matches the assessment language. Fix: thinking-language vs writing-language field. AURA can scaffold in the learner's thinking language if requested, then help them translate into the assessment language.
WEAKSecondary learners doing senior exams (HSC, VCE, IB): time-pressured, high-stakes, often memorisation-heavy. AURA's Pareto model is task-based not exam-preparation based. No exam revision mode. Fix: exam prep mode. Replaces Pareto Steps with spaced repetition and retrieval practice protocols. Socratic questions become active recall prompts.
MISSINGLearners in remote or low-connectivity areas: AURA assumes consistent internet. A student in remote Australia on satellite internet with 2-second latency will have voice mode break, API calls timeout, and the whole guidance experience degrade silently. Fix: offline/low-bandwidth mode. Core AURA functions degrade gracefully: voice off, responses pre-cached where possible, explicit "working offline" indicator.
MISSINGNight-shift workers, carers, and people studying at 3am: AURA has no time-of-day awareness. A carer studying at 3am after a shift has a completely different cognitive load profile than the same person at 9am. Fix: time-of-day context injected into runtime. If session opens between 10pm and 5am, AURA defaults to lower cognitive load scaffolding without being told to.
MISSINGLearners who are parents or carers (interrupted study): session may be interrupted by a child crying, a care need, an emergency. AURA has no fast-save and fast-return protocol. Fix: panic-save button. One tap saves full state with a timestamp. On return: "You left during [block]. Here is exactly where you were."
Pass 3 Institutional and real-world context stress test 11 gaps found
MISSINGAcademic integrity investigation: a learner is being investigated by their institution for suspected AI misuse. AURA has no protocol for a learner asking "can I show them my Authenticity Report as proof I did this work?" The report needs to be exportable in a format institutions accept. Fix: Authenticity Report export as a signed, tamper-evident PDF with timestamp and session log summary.
MISSINGInstitutional policy conflict: a university bans AI tools entirely. A learner using Simplifii at that institution is violating policy. AURA does not acknowledge this tension. It should. Fix: AURA is transparent about what Simplifii does. If a learner asks "is using this allowed?", AURA says: "That depends on your institution's policy. Check your course outline. Simplifii logs all AI assistance so you can show your process if asked."
MISSINGAssignment extensions and accommodations: a learner with a disability needs to request an extension. AURA knows the deadline. It does not help the learner navigate extension requests, special consideration forms, or disability support services. This is core hidden curriculum. Fix: extension scaffolding protocol. When time_remaining is critically low and past_harm_signal or disability_disclosures is present, AURA surfaces: "You may be eligible for an extension. Want help drafting the request?"
MISSINGLearner changes institution mid-year: all their rubrics, timelines, and Pareto Steps are tied to the old institution's format. AURA does not handle institution migration. Fix: institution context field in profile. When changed, AURA flags all tasks as needing rubric re-confirmation and walks the learner through updating them.
MISSINGGroup assignments: AURA's entire model assumes solo authorship. Group tasks have different rubric structures, different authenticity splits, and different Pareto Step logic. No group mode. Fix: group assignment flag. Pareto Steps adapt to individual contribution requirements. Authenticity split tracks each contributor separately. Socratic questions address the learner's specific section.
MISSINGOral presentation and non-written assessments: AURA scaffolds written work. A learner preparing a 10-minute oral presentation has no guidance path in the current model. Fix: presentation mode. Pareto Steps map to speaking points not blocks. Tier 2 Socratic questions become rehearsal prompts. Tier 3 is a speaker notes editor.
MISSINGLearner experiencing homelessness or housing instability: no fixed study space, no reliable device, may be using a library computer with 30-minute session limit. AURA assumes personal device ownership and stable access. Fix: shared/public device mode. No data persists locally. All state lives in cloud. Session timeout warning at 25 minutes.
WEAKFinancial stress: a learner who is food-insecure or financially stressed cannot focus. AURA detects cognitive load signals but not financial distress signals. When a learner mentions cost, money, or not being able to afford something, AURA should know to surface student support resources. Fix: financial distress signal detection. AURA surfaces student support/financial assistance resources once, without judgment.
MISSINGLearner works full-time alongside study: time poverty is the primary constraint, not ability. AURA's LOD = Sprint should know the difference between a learner who is disengaged and one who has 20 minutes and needs the single highest-value action, no scaffolding, no Socratic detours. Fix: time-poverty mode. When focus_window_minutes is below 30, AURA defaults to absolute minimum viable guidance: one action, no questions, fast.
MISSINGLearner fails a course and must repeat it: all prior Pareto Steps, rubric data, and session history are from a failed attempt. AURA should not pretend the failure did not happen, but also should not open with it. The prior attempt contains useful data (what the weakest criteria were). Fix: repeat-course mode. AURA loads the gap analysis from the prior attempt as the primary Pareto input. Prior failure is framed as a diagnostic, not a verdict.
MISSINGLearner defers mid-semester due to medical withdrawal: returns months later to the same course. All deadlines have passed. The timeline is broken. AURA has no re-entry protocol for deferred students. Fix: deferral re-entry protocol. On return, AURA asks: "You are returning after a break. Your previous deadlines are no longer current. Want to update your timeline before we start?"
Pass 4 Voice, language, and communication edge cases 9 gaps found
MISSINGMultilingual internal monologue: a learner thinks in Mandarin, writes in English, and AURA responds in English. They may need to process AURA's guidance in their thinking language before acting on it. AURA has no bilingual scaffolding mode. Fix: thinking-language field. If set, AURA can offer key guidance in both languages simultaneously when requested.
MISSINGScreen reader users: AURA's output includes response format structures (rubric check lists, LOD=Map tables) that may be linearised badly by screen readers. The output format itself needs to be screen-reader-optimised, not just the UI. Fix: screen-reader output mode. All structured AURA outputs are reformatted as clean numbered prose instead of tables or visual lists when screen_reader is true.
MISSINGBraille display users: character limits per line matter. Long AURA responses that wrap badly on a Braille display disrupt comprehension. No Braille-optimised output mode. Fix: Braille mode. Response segments at 40-character line lengths. No long compound sentences.
MISSINGLow-vision learners: not fully blind, not standard-sighted. Needs large print and high contrast but can still read. The current a11y vault has contrast and font size but no large-print mode that reflows the entire AURA output layout. Fix: large-print mode. All AURA output renders at 150% base font with increased line spacing and single-column layout.
MISSINGDeaf and hard-of-hearing learners: voice mode is inaccessible. TTS output is inaccessible. But also: academic language that is built on spoken language patterns may be harder to acquire for learners whose first language is Auslan or another sign language. No signing-community academic language support. Fix: Deaf profile. Voice input/output suppressed entirely. Academic English modelled explicitly as a second language register, not assumed as a first language base.
WEAKCALD learners (culturally and linguistically diverse) with different educational epistemologies: some cultures have entirely different frameworks for what "argument" or "critical analysis" means. AURA teaches the Western academic rubric standard as if it is universal. Fix: AURA explicitly names that it is teaching one particular academic tradition and why institutions require it. Never presents it as the only valid way to think.
MISSINGFirst Nations learners with different relationships to knowledge ownership and sharing: Western academic citation norms can conflict with Indigenous knowledge protocols (who can speak this knowledge, under what conditions). AURA has no cultural knowledge sovereignty awareness. Fix: cultural knowledge flag. AURA notes when a task may involve knowledge the learner has cultural authority over and checks how they want to handle attribution.
MISSINGLearners with severe math anxiety: not dyscalculia but anxiety-based avoidance. Will shut down when AURA surfaces statistics, data, or quantitative rubric criteria. No math anxiety detection or specific de-escalation path. Fix: quantitative anxiety protocol. When math-adjacent rubric criteria are surfaced, AURA breaks them into the smallest non-numeric steps first before introducing any numbers.
MISSINGLearners with severe writing anxiety: blank page paralysis that is anxiety-driven, not executive-function-driven. These are different. An ADHD blank-page issue responds to Tier 1 pre-write. An anxiety-blank-page needs the anxiety addressed first. Fix: writing anxiety detection. If idle after Tier 1 generation is offered and the learner has not engaged with it, AURA asks: "Is it the writing itself that feels hard right now, or are you not sure what to write?" Different answers get different responses.
Pass 5 System integrity and failure modes: what happens when AURA itself breaks 8 gaps found
MISSINGAURA gives a wrong Pareto Step: the ingestion confidence was low, the rubric was misread, and AURA sends the learner to work on a section worth 10% instead of the section worth 40%. Learner trusts AURA and loses marks. AURA has no self-correction protocol when its own guidance is wrong. Fix: confidence watermark on every Pareto Step. "Step 1 (confidence: high / medium / low - based on rubric clarity)." Low confidence steps include: "I am less certain about this one. Check your rubric."
MISSINGAURA misidentifies a crisis signal: a learner says "I want to kill this essay" and AURA triggers a safety protocol. False positives on safety detection destroy trust and patronise the learner. Fix: safety signal clarification protocol. Before escalating to full crisis mode, AURA checks once: "Are you okay, or just frustrated with the work?" One question. If "frustrated" then acknowledge and continue. If no response or a distress signal, escalate.
MISSINGAURA loops: a Socratic question receives a non-answer, AURA asks a smaller question, receives another non-answer, spirals to an impossibly small question that still cannot be answered. No exit from the Socratic spiral. Fix: maximum 3 Socratic depth levels. After the third level, AURA exits Socratic mode and switches to Literal Assistant for that block only: "I will give you a starting point for this one."
MISSINGProfile drift: a learner's actual needs change significantly over a semester (diagnosis received, medication changed, life circumstances shift) but the profile is not updated. AURA becomes increasingly misaligned. No profile recalibration trigger. Fix: quarterly profile check-in prompt (not mandatory). "Your profile was set 90 days ago. Want to update anything?" Plus: learner can update any field at any time from Settings without going through full onboarding again.
MISSINGAURA is used maliciously: a parent, partner, or institution forces access to a learner's Simplifii account to surveil them. AURA has no awareness of or protection against coerced use. Fix: private note feature. A learner can leave a note only they can see (not even in the Authenticity Report). Plus: AURA notices if access patterns suggest shared or supervised use and asks privately: "Are you using this freely, or is someone watching?"
MISSINGAURA over-scaffolds a capable learner: a highly capable learner with Scaffolding = Heavy (set from an early session when they were overwhelmed) is being over-helped months later. AURA never suggests reducing scaffolding based on observed capability growth. Fix: scaffolding growth detection. After 10 sessions where the learner rarely needs Tier 1 pre-write, AURA suggests: "You have been working independently more often. Want to try the Light scaffolding setting?"
MISSINGSimultaneous multi-device use: learner opens Simplifii on their phone and laptop at the same time. AURA state conflicts. Two active sessions, two context objects, potentially contradictory guidance. Fix: last-write-wins with session conflict warning. If two sessions are detected: "It looks like you have Simplifii open in two places. Which one are you working in right now?"
MISSINGAURA is asked to do something unethical by a learner who frames it as a legitimate request: "write my whole essay and I will edit it after" framed as "generate a full draft in Tier 1 for me to adapt." AURA's Tier 1 spec says frames and starters only. But there is no explicit rule for when the learner pushes past the limit creatively. Fix: explicit Tier 1 ceiling in the prompt. AURA can generate a maximum of one paragraph per block as a starter frame. Any request for more is declined with the reason: "A full draft is not something I can write for you. Here is why that would work against you: [one sentence referencing the rubric or authenticity report]."
