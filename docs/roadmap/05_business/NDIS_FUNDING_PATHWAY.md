## Why this matters

The NDIS spent $43 billion in 2024-25. Capacity Building budgets within individual plans typically range $5K-$15K/year. Disability-aligned digital tools that genuinely support participants can be fundable.

If Sovereign Maths (Autistic Learners) and Sovereign Home (Homeschool for ND families) become NDIS-fundable:
- Participants pay nothing out of pocket
- Plan managers / support coordinators recommend Sovereign
- Volume grows without consumer-marketing burn
- Revenue is reliable (NDIS pays on invoice)

## Which products are NDIS-eligible candidates

### Strong candidates
- **Sovereign Maths for Autistic Learners** — clearly addresses an "Improved Learning" outcome for autistic participants
- **Sovereign Home (Homeschool)** for families where the child has a disability diagnosis and traditional schooling failed
- **Sovereign Study (HSC)** for Year 11-12 participants with disability diagnoses

### Weak candidates
- Generic Undergrad / Research / Academic tiers (NDIS funds support for daily life and capacity-building, not generic productivity)

### Possible specific carve-outs
- **Sovereign Pathways** for participants accessing tertiary education with disability
- **Communications Layer standalone** if framed as "executive function and communication support"

## NDIS framework basics

NDIS Capacity Building category includes:
- CB Daily Activity (life skills support)
- CB Health and Wellbeing
- CB Relationships
- CB Social and Community Participation
- **CB Improved Learning** ← our target
- CB Improved Living Arrangements
- CB Employment

Within "Improved Learning", supports must:
- Be related to the participant's disability
- Help build skills for ongoing learning
- Represent "reasonable and necessary" support
- Provide value for money
- Be evidence-based or trial-able

## Funding mechanisms

### Path 1: Direct participant pay
Participant has Capacity Building funding in their plan. They pay Sovereign directly, then claim back via:
- Plan-managed: their plan manager pays Sovereign from NDIS funds
- Self-managed: participant pays Sovereign, claims reimbursement
- Agency-managed: more complex, Sovereign must be NDIS-registered

### Path 2: Plan manager pays directly
Sovereign invoices the plan manager monthly. Plan manager pays from participant's plan funding.

### Path 3: Support coordinator recommends, family chooses
Support coordinator includes Sovereign as a recommended support in the participant's plan. Family signs up. Plan manager pays.

### Path 4: NDIS-registered provider status
Sovereign becomes NDIS-registered provider:
- Higher administrative burden
- Can serve agency-managed participants (largest segment)
- Quality and Safeguarding Commission compliance required
- Annual audit costs ~$10K-$30K
- Worth it once we have 100+ NDIS-funded users

## Evidence required

NDIS requires evidence that supports work. For Sovereign:

### Internal evidence (to build over Year 1)
- User testimonials from participant families
- Pre/post engagement metrics
- Pre/post learning outcome measures (literacy gains, numeracy gains)
- Wellbeing measures (reduced anxiety, improved mood)
- Parent satisfaction surveys

### External evidence (to publish)
- Pilot study with disability-led research partner (Aria Learning is perfect collaborator)
- Peer-reviewed publication on effectiveness
- Independent evaluation report
- Cost-effectiveness analysis (vs alternative supports)

## Pricing for NDIS

Different pricing structure from consumer:
- **Quoted per hour of support equivalent** (NDIS prefers this framing)
- **Sovereign Maths Individual:** equivalent to ~30 minutes of therapy support per month → $80-100/month
- **Sovereign Home Family:** equivalent to ~1 hour of support coordinator time per month → $120-150/month
- Higher than consumer pricing because justified by therapeutic outcome, not productivity

NDIS Price Guide compliance required (annual update, max rates per support type).

## Risks

### Risk: NDIS reform
NDIS undergoing constant policy reform. "Foundational supports" reform may shift what's fundable. Mitigation: maintain consumer revenue base; don't bet everything on NDIS.

### Risk: Administrative burden
NDIS reporting and compliance can be heavy. Mitigation: hire NDIS-experienced operations person before scaling.

### Risk: Provider registration costs
NDIS-registered provider audit costs ~$30K initially + ongoing. Mitigation: only register when revenue justifies (100+ users).

### Risk: Evidence requirements
Without published evidence, "experimental" tools get denied. Mitigation: Aaron's PhD research becomes the evidence base.
