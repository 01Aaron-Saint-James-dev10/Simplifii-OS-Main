# Target Segments [SPEC]

## What this is

The Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) per Sovereign mode. Who exactly we're building for. Specific enough to write copy, run ads, choose channels, design features.

## Status

## How to use this doc

When designing a new feature, ask: "Which ICP does this serve?" If the answer isn't immediate, the feature is probably scope creep.

When writing marketing copy, ask: "Which ICP am I speaking to?" Then write to that one person specifically.

## SOVEREIGN RESEARCH ICPs

### ICP-R1: The Neurodivergent MRes / PhD Candidate (PRIMARY)
- **Age:** 22-45
- **Gender:** Mixed, slightly female-skewed (autism/ADHD diagnosis underdiagnosed in women but rising)
- **Location:** Major Australian cities + regional university towns (Newcastle, Wollongong, Townsville)
- **Education:** Currently enrolled in Honours, MRes, PhD; previously completed Bachelor's
- **Income:** Often Centrelink + scholarship; lean budget; price-sensitive but values quality
- **Diagnoses:** ADHD, autism, dyslexia, dyscalculia, often comorbidities
- **Tech stack:** Mac (Apple ecosystem heavy), iPhone, Notion or Obsidian for notes, Zotero for refs, Word/Pages for writing
- **Pain points:**
  - Existing tools (Word, Grammarly, ChatGPT) erase voice
  - Citation chaos
  - Methodology decision tracking
  - Reflexivity / positionality work invisible to standard tools
  - Imposter syndrome amplified by tools designed for neurotypical workflows
  - Supervisor communication anxiety
- **What they read:** Substack, Twitter / X academic community, LinkedIn occasionally
- **What they spend money on:** Coffee, books, productivity tools, therapy, supplements
- **Why they'd pay for Sovereign:** Their thesis success is existential. Tools that genuinely help are worth $39/month.

### ICP-R2: The First-in-Family Researcher
- **Age:** 25-50
- **Background:** First in family to do postgraduate study; working-class, immigrant, or care-experienced
- **Tech stack:** Mixed; less prior productivity-tool sophistication
- **Pain points:**
  - Imposter syndrome
  - Lack of insider language
  - Supervisor expectation confusion
  - Limited peer network in academia
- **Why they'd pay:** Sovereign Pathways elements within Research mode = literacy coach + jargon decoder.

### ICP-R3: The Senior Academic Power User
- **Age:** 35-65
- **Role:** Senior lecturer, associate professor, professor
- **Output:** 5-15 publications/year, multiple grants, co-authoring
- **Pain points:**
  - Multi-publication coordination
  - Co-author chaos
  - Journal target tracking
  - Peer review response drudgery
- **Why they'd pay:** Time savings on admin work. Premium tier $149/month is institutional-level pricing they can expense.

### ICP-R4: The Late-Career Returner
- **Age:** 50-70
- **Story:** Returning to study after career; doing MRes or PhD as second act
- **Pain points:**
  - Out of touch with current academic tooling
  - Voice DNA matters intensely (they have a strong voice from prior career)
  - Slower adoption curve
- **Why they'd pay:** Voice preservation + structured scaffolding = re-entry made dignified.

## SOVEREIGN STUDY ICPs

### ICP-S1: The High-Performing Neurodivergent Year 12 (PRIMARY)
- **Age:** 17-18
- **Location:** NSW metro + regional
- **School:** Public selective, private, public comp
- **Diagnoses:** ADHD, autism, dyslexia (often undiagnosed but symptomatic)
- **Pain points:**
  - Knows content, can't translate to exam format
  - Anxiety + perfectionism cycle
  - Parents stressed, can't help
- **What they use:** Atomic Habits, Anki, YouTube tutorials, ATAR Notes, Reddit r/HSC
- **What they'd pay for:** Exam format mastery; ATAR aspirations; their parents pay $20/month happily.

### ICP-S2: The Late-Starter Year 12
- **Age:** 17-18
- **Story:** Realised in term 3 that HSC is real, panicking
- **Pain points:** Topic gaps, time pressure, anxiety
- **What they'd pay for:** Crunch Pack ($99 one-time term 4 access) makes sense.

### ICP-S3: The Equity Pathway Year 12
- **Background:** Low-SES, first-in-family, refugee/migrant, Indigenous, ESL
- **Pain points:** Lack of context, vocabulary, confidence, family support
- **What they need:** Free tier funded by scholarship partnerships (Smith Family, Schools Plus)
- **Why this matters:** Mission-critical. Aaron's MRes is literally about this gap.

### ICP-S4: The Parent of HSC Student
- **Age:** 40-55
- **Story:** Year 12 kid stressed, parent can't help with content, wants to support
- **Pain points:** Doesn't understand HSC syllabus; relationship strain over study
- **What they'd pay for:** Family plan $35/month to access parent dashboard and explanation tools.

## SOVEREIGN PATHWAYS ICPs

### ICP-P1: The Indigenous Pathway Student
- **Background:** First-in-family Indigenous student in pre-university pathway
- **Pain points:** Cultural code-switching; imposter syndrome; voice erasure by AI tools
- **What they need:** Cultural responsiveness; legitimisation of Indigenous knowledge systems
- **Pricing:** Free via partnership with universities' Indigenous units / Aboriginal Land Council partners

### ICP-P2: The Refugee Background Student
- **Background:** Recent migrant, often qualified professional in home country; bridging to Australian credentials
- **Pain points:** Academic English; mother tongue thinking; trauma-informed support
- **What they need:** Mother tongue drafting mode; respectful trauma-informed scaffolding
- **Pricing:** Free via partnership with refugee resettlement agencies

### ICP-P3: The First-in-Family Bridging Student
- **Background:** Working-class, no university family network
- **Pain points:** Doesn't know how to email lecturers; supervisor expectations opaque; library resources daunting
- **What they need:** Communications Layer; supervisor expectation explainers
- **Pricing:** Free via university widening-participation funds (HEPPP)

### ICP-P4: The Mature-Age Returner
- **Age:** 30-60
- **Background:** Returning to education after career interruption
- **Pain points:** Out of practice; family/work juggling; tech intimidation
- **What they need:** Confidence-building copy; clear navigation; minimal jargon
- **Pricing:** Subsidised tier $15/month

## ICP rejection list (who we're NOT for)

### Not our customer:
- Mainstream "productivity hacks" seekers
- ChatGPT power users wanting more AI generation
- Students wanting to cheat (Receipt explicitly prevents this)
- Universities wanting student surveillance tools (we protect students)
- Corporate L&D programmes (different product, different market)
- Casual writers without academic goals (Notion/Obsidian serve them fine)

### Not our market (for now):
- US undergraduates (too crowded, different competitor stack)
- K-6 (different cognitive needs, different market structure)
- Adult literacy programmes (different product entirely)
- ESL learners without academic context (general language apps better fit)
