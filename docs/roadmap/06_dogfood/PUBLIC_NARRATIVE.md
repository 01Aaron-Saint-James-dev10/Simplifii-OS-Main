# Public Narrative — Build-in-Public Strategy [SPEC]

## What this is

How Sovereign OS is told to the world. Not advertising. Story. The build-in-public narrative that turns Aaron's MRes into Sovereign's strongest asset.

## Status

[SPEC] — already partially happening on Aaron's LinkedIn. Formalise.

## The core story

> A neurodivergent MRes researcher, frustrated by writing tools that erase his voice and can't prove he did the work, builds the research tool he wishes existed. Writes his thesis on it. Publishes the receipt. Lets other researchers see the workings.

That's it. That's the story. Every post, every conference talk, every podcast appearance threads back to this.

## What this narrative is NOT

- Not a startup founder hustle story
- Not "I'm going to disrupt academia"
- Not aggressive marketing copy
- Not techno-optimism
- Not "AI will save education"

The energy is: **a researcher building a tool, transparently, with his own neurodivergent cognition as the design constraint.**

## Narrative pillars

### Pillar 1: Authenticity over performance
Aaron writes in his own voice. Australian English. No em-dashes. Specific. Direct. No corporate gloss.

When something fails, Aaron says so. When something works, Aaron shows the data (not hypes it).

When Sovereign helps, Aaron narrates it. When Sovereign frustrates, Aaron writes a feature spec instead of complaining.

### Pillar 2: Neurodivergence as design ground truth
Aaron's ADHD, dyslexia, and lived experience are not "obstacles he overcame." They're the design lens.

Every feature has a "because Aaron's brain needed this" origin. The Brain Dump, the Body Double, the Sensory Profile, the Friction Mode — each has a story.

This positions Sovereign not as "accessible accommodations bolted on" but as "designed-from-the-start for variation."

### Pillar 3: Research as practice, not theatre
Aaron's MRes isn't content. It's actual work. Methodology decisions are real. Reflexivity tensions are real. Supervisor feedback is real.

Public posts reveal process, not findings. "Here's how I decided X" not "Here's what I discovered" (until published).

### Pillar 4: Equity as spine
Sovereign serves the students the system forgot. This is not a feature; it's the moral foundation.

Every product launch announces the equity tier alongside the premium tier. Always.

### Pillar 5: Sovereignty over surveillance
Local-first. Privacy-first. User-owned data. No ads. No surveillance. No exploitation.

This is unusual in the AI tools space. Lean into it as differentiation.

## Channels and cadence

### LinkedIn (primary)
- **2-3 posts per week** during build phase
- **3-4 posts per week** during launch phases
- Mix of: process insights, milestone announcements, feature demos, reflexivity moments, community engagement
- Optimised for algorithm: 8:15 AM AEDT post time, 3-5 niche hashtags, no external links in post body, PDF carousels for high-engagement
- Always end with a question or invitation (not a CTA)

### Substack newsletter
- **Monthly** longer-form essays
- 2,000-4,000 words
- Topics rotate: process, methodology, sector commentary, product reflection
- Free tier; paid tier later if community wants it

### Twitter / X
- Lower priority but worth maintaining presence
- Cross-post key threads
- Engage in #AcademicTwitter, #PhDChat, #ADHDAcademic conversations
- Less polished than LinkedIn; more conversational

### Instagram (later, for Sovereign Home / Sovereign Study)
- Image-led storytelling
- Behind-the-scenes from Aaron's build

- Parents-of-HSC engagement (when Sovereign Study launches)

### Podcast appearances
- **1-2 per month** when product is shipping
- Curated: prioritise listener overlap with target ICPs
- Always offer specific takeaways, not pitch
- Recording = repurpose-able content (quote graphics, audio clips, transcript posts)

### Conference talks
- **2-3 per year minimum**
- HERDSA, AARE, ASCILITE for academic audiences

- Always submit as research talks (not product pitches), let the product context emerge organically

## Voice DNA for posts

Aaron's writing rules apply to all public content:
- Australian English (never US)
- No em-dashes
- Direct, specific, no hype
- No corporate jargon
- No AI-tell words ("delve", "tapestry", "navigate")
- Confident without arrogance
- Critical without bitterness
- Personal without oversharing

Public posts go through Sovereign's Voice DNA service when built. Aaron approves before posting.

## Risks

### Risk: Imposter syndrome amplified by public posting
Mitigation: Aaron has a community (DMs to specific friends when wobbly). Therapy / coaching as needed. This is a real cost of build-in-public.

### Risk: Sector backlash (someone takes offense to a post)
Mitigation: post in good faith. When wrong, acknowledge. When right and critical, stand by it. Sector politics are real but rarely catastrophic if integrity is intact.

### Risk: Time spent posting > time spent building
Mitigation: posting is part of the work, but cap it. 1-2 hours per week max. Use Sovereign's own features (Voice DNA, Communications Layer) to draft posts faster.

### Risk: Saying too much pre-publication
Mitigation: discipline. Process is public. Findings stay private until published.

### Risk: Narrative becomes the product
Mitigation: build the product first, narrate it second. If a feature isn't working, no amount of narrative saves it.
